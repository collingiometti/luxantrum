SOCIETY

Before the revelare, the absence of ethnic and religous diversity within the empire left space for the separation of districts to form social classes. At the very top was the famed council, followed by officials, with religious figures and scholars also regarded quite highly. At the bottom were the laborers such as farmers and miners. These classes were not so rigid, however, as children born into their districts had the opportunity to switch, and even advance into positions of power.

The revelare introduced the empire to diversity, significantly shifting social structures. At first, the original luxantrians had more opportunities and power, though this gradually died as diversity normalized. However, the minor separations between districts remained continuous.
